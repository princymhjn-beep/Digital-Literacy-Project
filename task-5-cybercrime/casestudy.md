## **Part A – Case Study (Phishing Example)**

**Cybercrime Type:** Phishing  
 **Word Count:** \~230 words

**Case Study: Phishing Attack on College Students**

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick people into revealing sensitive information like passwords, banking details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. One common method is via emails or messages that look official.

**How it typically happens:**

1. The attacker sends a fake email or WhatsApp message claiming to be from a bank, university portal, or UPI app.  
2. The message often creates urgency, like “Your account will be locked” or “You need to verify your UPI immediately.”  
3. The victim clicks the provided link, which leads to a fake login page that looks legitimate.  
4. When the victim enters credentials or OTPs, the attacker collects them instantly.  
5. The attacker can now use the stolen details to steal money or access private accounts.

**Who is usually targeted:** College students, young professionals, and anyone who frequently uses online banking or UPI apps. Students are especially vulnerable because they may be less cautious about checking URLs or verifying sources.

**Consequences:** Victims can lose money, have bank accounts drained, or suffer identity theft. Emotional stress and fear of sharing financial details online are common side effects. Phishing can also lead to further attacks if attackers access email or social media accounts

**Source for real incident (optional):** Times of India reported a case in 2022 where multiple college students lost funds through fake UPI links.
